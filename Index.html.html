Origin

I grew up in Pakistan — a place that taught me to be resourceful, fast, and never wait for perfect conditions. In a Jamali household, ambition was expected and family was constant. Both shaped how I work: with urgency, with loyalty, and with a strong sense that things should actually get done.

My first real encounter with computers wasn't about games — it was about the feeling that the screen was a window into a different kind of thinking. By the time I was in secondary school, I knew I wanted to build things, not just use them.

the turn

The moment I discovered machine learning, something clicked. The idea that you could show a model enough examples and it would learn the pattern on its own — that felt like real magic. I enrolled in Data Science at University of Sindh in 2024, joined SMIT's Deep Learning program and PITP's Data Science program simultaneously, and just started building. SpamScan was my first serious model. 97% F1. Live on Hugging Face. That was the proof of concept that changed everything.

the work

I've built CNNs, fine-tuned transformer models, done EDA on tens of thousands of data points, and shipped multiple React.js web applications for real clients at HayViral. Every project taught me something the lecture notes didn't. I care more about what ships than what looks impressive on paper.

now

I'm actively building in public, studying full-time across three programs simultaneously, and genuinely excited about where all of this goes. I want to work on AI systems that matter — not demos, not toys, but things that change how someone does their job or lives their day. Available for AI Engineer roles, remote or on-site, worldwide. If you've read this far, reach out.
